# Visitor-facing copy for Humpback Whale Migration

Audience: general public, optimized for ~8-year-old reading level.
Voice: short sentences, high visual impact, no jargon.

## Title

Humpback Whale Migration

## Short caption

Humpback whales travel thousands of miles every year — and their journey helps the whole ocean.

## Scenes

Each scene is **15 seconds**. Total loop: **90 seconds**.

### Scene 1 — The Epic Journey (0–15s)

Humpback whales travel up to 5,000 miles every year! That is like walking across the USA and back.

### Scene 2 — The Cold Buffet (15–30s)

Summer in the Polar Seas: Time to eat! Whales gulp down thousands of pounds of tiny krill to build up thick blubber.

### Scene 3 — The Warm Nursery (30–45s)

Winter in the Tropics: Time for family! Mothers give birth in warm waters so their babies stay safe and warm.

### Scene 4 — The Ocean's Gardeners (45–60s)

Why migration matters: As whales travel, they mix up ocean nutrients. This helps tiny sea plants grow, which gives us oxygen to breathe!

### Scene 5 — The Journey's Dangers (60–75s)

A Risky Trip: Whales must dodge big ships, plastic pollution, and tangled fishing nets on their long walk through the waves.

### Scene 6 — Be a Whale Hero (75–90s)

You can help! Keep beaches clean, use less plastic, and protect our blue planet. Enjoy the exhibit!

## Call to action

Keep beaches clean, use less plastic, and protect our blue planet.
