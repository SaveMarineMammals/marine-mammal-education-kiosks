# Visitor-facing copy for Seagrass for Manatees

Audience: general public, optimized for ~8-year-old reading level.
Voice: scannable bullets, plain text only for CMS (no Markdown, no emoji).
Layout: Glance & Match (Zone A static · Zone B art+copy · Zone C ticker).

## Zone A — Hero identification (static)

West Indian Manatee  
Trichechus manatus

| Metric | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Quick ID | Gentle grazer · seagrass eater · slow swimmer |
| Max size | About 13 feet |
| Weight | About 1,200 pounds |
| Status | Vulnerable |

## Zone B — Paging insight cards (6s each · 24s loop)

Art sits in the upper band; copy sits in the lower band (never over the graphic).

### Card 1 — Food

- Manatees graze seagrass like cows on land
- One adult can eat 100 pounds a day
- Healthy meadows keep manatees fed

### Card 2 — Threat

- Boats and dredging tear up seagrass beds
- Cloudy water blocks the sun plants need
- Lost meadows mean less food for manatees

### Card 3 — Hidden Fact

- Seagrass holds sand and clears the water
- Baby fish hide in the blades to grow
- Meadows store carbon and slow storms

### Card 4 — How to Help

- Go slow in marked manatee zones
- Keep trash and fertilizer out of water
- Share why seagrass meadows matter
